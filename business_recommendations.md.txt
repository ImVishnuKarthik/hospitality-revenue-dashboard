# Hospitality Revenue Analysis — Business Recommendations
**Dataset:** Fast Food Sales Report | 1,000 Transactions | April 2022 – March 2023

---

## Executive Summary
Analysis of 1,000 transactions across a 12-month period reveals clear patterns 
in revenue by time of day, day of week, product category, and seasonality. 
The following recommendations are designed to help the business maximise revenue 
during peak periods and recover lost revenue during seasonal dips.

---

## Key Findings & Recommendations

### 1. Night Trading Drives the Most Revenue
**Finding:** Night is the highest revenue-generating time period, 
significantly outperforming Morning sales.

**Recommendation:**
- Concentrate staffing and stock levels during night shifts
- Introduce night-specific promotions or combo deals to further 
  capitalise on high footfall
- Investigate why Morning underperforms — consider revised opening 
  hours or a breakfast menu to capture untapped demand

---

### 2. Significant Seasonal Dips in April and June
**Finding:** April (6.4%) and June (6.3%) are the two weakest months, 
generating nearly 40% less revenue than the peak month of January (10.4%).

**Recommendation:**
- Launch targeted promotions in April and June — meal deals, 
  loyalty discounts, or limited-time offers
- Use quieter months for staff training, menu refresh, and 
  equipment maintenance to minimise disruption during peak periods
- Investigate external factors driving the dip — weather, local 
  events, competition — and respond proactively

---

### 3. January and October Are Peak Opportunities
**Finding:** January (10.4%) and October (9.9%) are the two strongest 
months combined accounting for over 20% of annual revenue.

**Recommendation:**
- Ensure maximum staffing, stock, and operational readiness 
  entering January and October
- Introduce seasonal menu items or limited offers to further 
  drive revenue during these natural peaks
- Use these months to trial new products and gather customer feedback 
  when footfall is highest

---

### 4. Cash Still Dominates but Online is Growing
**Finding:** 53% of transactions are cash, 47% online — 
nearly an even split with 107 unrecorded transactions.

**Recommendation:**
- Prioritise fixing transaction recording gaps — 107 unknown 
  transactions represent lost business intelligence
- Actively encourage online/card payments — faster service, 
  better data capture, reduced cash handling risk
- Analyse whether online payers spend more per transaction 
  than cash payers — this could inform a digital-first strategy

---

### 5. Menu Optimisation Opportunity
**Finding:** Top 10 items drive a disproportionate share of revenue 
while lower performing items take up menu space and stock cost.

**Recommendation:**
- Promote top performing items more prominently — on menus, 
  signage, and any digital ordering platforms
- Review bottom performing items for removal or reformulation 
  to reduce waste and simplify operations
- Consider bundling top items into meal deals to increase 
  average transaction value
